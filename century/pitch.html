02 / Root Cause

The system measures yield per acre. Nobody measures nutrition per acre.

Agricultural infrastructure—from seed breeding to soil testing to crop insurance—was built to optimize one variable: tons per acre. Nutrient density isn't tracked, priced, or rewarded anywhere in the supply chain.

  • Seed companies breed for yield, shelf life, and pest resistance. Nutrient density is not a selection variable.
  • Soil testing measures N-P-K for growth. Micronutrients, trace minerals, and soil biology are rarely measured at field scale.
  • Crop insurance prices risk on satellite imagery and county averages. A $20B federal program that has never seen a soil sensor reading.
  • Carbon markets need field-level verification. Periodic lab samples at $25–50 each can't cover a 5,000-acre operation.

The missing piece isn't agronomy knowledge. It's the physical infrastructure to measure what matters—continuously, underground, at field scale, at a price point that works.

05 / Why Now

Three structural forces converging.

Water Crisis

California's SGMA will force 500K–1M acres out of production by 2040. Water districts legally need field-level monitoring data—and the sensors don't exist yet. Central Valley land has bifurcated 8–10x: water-secure ($12–23K/acre) vs. water-insecure ($2–3K/acre).

Generational Transfer

Average farmer age: 58.3. 40% of US farmland ($1.5T) changing hands by 2035. Only 1 in 4 farms has a succession plan. New operators will adopt technology—they don't have 30 years of intuition to rely on.

Regulatory Demand

EU Data Act mandates agricultural equipment data portability. 7 US states passed right-to-repair for ag equipment (2023–2025). Carbon credit markets require sensor-verified field data—no sensor, no credit.
