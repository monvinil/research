Why Underground Data Matters ‚Äî What Satellites Can and Cannot See

üîí Satellites Cannot Do This

  • Root-zone water ‚Äî the water crops actually use (30-120cm). Satellites see top 5cm only.
  • Soil nutrients ‚Äî nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium. Not observable from space.
  • Carbon measurement ‚Äî soil carbon is underground. No sensor = no verified credit.
  • Precision irrigation ‚Äî drip scheduling requires depth profiles, not surface estimates.

These four use cases drive the institutional revenue.

üõ∞Ô∏è Satellites Do This Well (and Getting Better)

  • Crop identification ‚Äî what's planted where. Satellite is excellent.
  • Surface moisture ‚Äî top 2-5cm. Good accuracy, free data.
  • Yield estimation ‚Äî 75-80% accuracy from space. Sensors push to 85-90%.
  • Vegetation health ‚Äî canopy temperature, growth stage. Well-covered.

Century uses satellites too ‚Äî the value is fusing both, not replacing one.
