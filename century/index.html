Competitive Moat ‚Äî What Satellites Can and Cannot See

üîí Underground: Satellites Physically Cannot Reach

  • Root-zone water ‚Äî the water crops actually draw on. Microwave satellites penetrate the top few centimeters of soil; the active root zone sits 30‚Äì150cm down depending on the crop.
  • Soil nutrients ‚Äî plant-available nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, and micronutrients. No electromagnetic wavelength from orbit measures these.
  • Soil carbon flux ‚Äî continuous below-ground measurement for carbon credit verification. The alternative is periodic lab sampling at $25‚Äì50/sample, which misses seasonal variation and is too expensive for field-scale coverage.
  • Depth-resolved irrigation ‚Äî precision drip and micro-sprinkler scheduling requires moisture profiles at multiple depths, not a single surface reading.

These four categories drive the highest-value institutional revenue streams.

üõ∞Ô∏è Surface & Canopy: Satellites Handle Well

  • Crop identification ‚Äî classifying what's planted where. Multispectral satellite imagery is mature and increasingly free.
  • Surface moisture ‚Äî top 2‚Äì5cm via SAR radar. Useful for rain-fed systems, less useful for irrigated agriculture where subsurface matters more.
  • Vegetation health ‚Äî canopy temperature, leaf area, growth stage. Well-covered by optical and thermal bands.
  • Yield estimation ‚Äî satellite-only models achieve roughly 75‚Äì80% accuracy on major row crops. Adding sensor data pushes accuracy to 85‚Äì90%. The gap sounds small, but on a $20B crop insurance market, 5‚Äì10% better pricing is worth billions.

Century uses satellite data as a layer in its platform ‚Äî the value is fusing surface, atmosphere, and subsurface, not replacing any one source.

Hardware Roadmap ‚Äî Why Custom Silicon, and How We Get There

Why not just keep buying off-the-shelf? Three reasons. First, cost: today's sensor node uses ~$40 in discrete analog and radio components that a single custom chip replaces for $2‚Äì3 at volume ‚Äî and unit cost keeps falling with production scale (Wright's Law). Second, power: on-chip machine learning decides what's worth transmitting, extending battery life from months to years. Third, form factor: smaller, sealed, and ruggedized for burial in soil. The custom chip isn't a luxury ‚Äî it's what makes blanket field coverage economically viable.
