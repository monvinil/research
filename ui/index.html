Research Engine

Cycle 0 · awaiting first run

What is this? An AI-powered research engine that maps the landscape of businesses likely to thrive in the AI economy over the next 3–5 years. It applies classical economic theories as operational lenses — Baumol, Schumpeter, Jevons, Perez, Polanyi — to detect structural forces and construct business models from the data up.

The engine uses a construct-first pipeline: Agent A scans data (FRED, BLS, EDGAR, O*NET, web) and extracts structural signals. Agent C grades signals by economic force, structural advantage, and timing readiness. Agent B constructs 5 business model types per opportunity — Direct Play, Structural Play, Jevons Expansion, Judgment-Premium (AGG), and Human-Premium (Baumol). Master synthesizes into a tiered landscape map with founder fit scored separately.

v2 engine: landscape-first, construct before filtering. Barriers are moats, not vetoes. Competitive density means the structural force is real. Three Polanyi categories per sector (routine-cognitive, tacit-manual, tacit-cognitive) yield three distinct business models, not one. Perez crash resilience check on every opportunity.
