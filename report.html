Research Methodology

This engine identifies structural economic shifts that create opportunities for agentic-first businesses. We reject industry-picking in favor of systemic pattern detection that reveals where the economy is structurally dislocating.

The Principles Engine: A Starting Framework, Not a Ceiling

The six principles below are initial scanning lenses, not a fixed taxonomy. They structure the first pass of data collection. But the real analytical value emerges from what happens between principles: second-order effects, cross-industry feedback loops, and emergent patterns that no single principle would surface alone.

As the research context deepens across cycles, the engine is expected to:

  • Discover new principles from data patterns that don't fit existing categories
  • Identify second-order effects — e.g., a P2 liquidation cascade in one industry creates a P5 demographic gap in an adjacent one
  • Surface cross-industry connections — distress in transportation + shortages in healthcare may point to an opportunity that neither signal reveals alone
  • Build reasoning context rich enough to make non-obvious inferences rather than pattern-matching against a fixed checklist

The goal of multi-cycle research is not "run the same scan more times." It's to build a context where the engine can reason about the economy with the depth an analyst would after months of immersion — seeing connections between moving pieces that no single data pull reveals.

Current Scanning Lenses (P1–P6)

P1 — Infrastructure Overhang Exploitation

When AI compute/models/tooling are overbuilt, returns shift to applications consuming cheap infrastructure. Look for businesses where the cost of intelligence has dropped below the threshold that killed previous attempts.

P2 — Liquidation Cascade Detection

Track the chain reaction: cheap AI → new entrants with 5–20x cost advantage → incumbent exit → asset liquidation → even cheaper entry. Enter at the point where assets (customers, contracts, licenses) become available cheaply.

P3 — Output Cost Kill

The winning move is not "better product" but identical output at radically lower cost. Model where agentic labor collapses the cost stack. The cost structure IS the moat.

P4 — Dead Business Revival

Businesses that died because labor/coordination costs exceeded revenue. With agentic ops, the break-even shifts. The market validated demand — only costs killed them.

P5 — Demographic Alignment

Structural labor gaps from aging, skill shortages, migration shifts. Agentic businesses filling these gaps get regulatory tailwinds, not headwinds.

P6 — Geopolitical Resilience

US-bloc viability, reshoring demand, LATAM corridor advantage from US-China decoupling.

Research Status & Context Development

Why multiple cycles matter: One inference cycle collects raw signals. It cannot reason about them with depth. The purpose of subsequent cycles is not just "more data" — it's to build an analytical context rich enough that the engine can identify non-obvious connections between the millions of moving pieces in the economy. Each cycle adds a new layer of context: quantitative baselines → qualitative practitioner signals → unit economics models → competitive landscape → cross-validated reasoning.
